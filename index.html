Taking pictures, especially good ones, can be a bit tricky at first. With a simple breakdown, it becomes much easier to understand!

Time to read: 5 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera
  • SD Card
  • Patience
  • Optional: Editing Software

  1. Familiarize Yourself With Your Camera

    Cameras tend to have quite a lot of buttons, switches, dials, and more. The easiest way to familiarize yourself with the controls of your camera is to look up the users manual or quickstart guide for your specific camera and take 10 minutes to learn all of the controls.

  2. Insert and Format your Card

    A SD card is needed to be able to save your photos. Some cameras and all phones will have built in memory to save your pictures but many handheld-dedicated digital or mirroless cameras will not. Insert your sd card into the camera and a prompt will more than likely show up asking if you'd like to format your card in which you will click yes. If this dialogue does not appear, navigate through settings and find format.

    >

  3. Change Camera Settings

    There are three main, important settings that most cameras will have: ISO, apeture, and shutter speed. This is commonly refered to as the "Exposure Traingle." The video which I have linked down below does a great job at explaining what each of these are responsible for. There is no one way to set up your camera as different conditions will warrant different setting. For example, you may want your apeture lower for a portrait, while you may want it higher for a landscape photo. The best way to find correct settings is experiment and see what works!

  4. Experiment!

    This is the most important step besides having access to a camera. Taking photos in the real world will be the only way to truly perfect your photography skills. Using a camera can be tricky, and each one differs in it's interface and how it operates but when you get used to it, it will be a breeze!

  5. Optional: Edit Your Photos

    You may decide to also edit your photos. This is crucial to get the exact type of look you want but is not required if you just want to capture memories and get decent photos. Many editing softwares and tutorials exist, I personally prefer photoshop as it provides endless controls to alter your photographs. One important thing to note is that some cameras may give you the option to shoot in "RAW." This is the most editable photo format and will provide you with the best results in the end.
